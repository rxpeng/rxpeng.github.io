Biography

I am a Ph. D. candidate majoring in Mass Communications, and minoring in Social Data Analytics. As a social and health psychologist, my research agenda is driven by a theoretical understanding of human behaviors that affect health, and the application of that knowledge to promote effective health behavior change at personal and societal level.

The primary topics of my research focus on the designs and effects of health promotion campaigns on social media. I also study how users interact with and understand digital health information. I envision my research expanding into the applications of virtual and augmented reality in healthcare considering its potential to make abstract public health concepts understandable, relevant, persuasive, and engaging.

I embrace diverse methodologies to better match the evolving media environment, including experimental design, survey research, sensors/tracking technologies and computational methods.
